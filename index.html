Radio

Radio waves are all around us and very useful.

  • Phones and tablets
  • Microwave ovens
  • Weather radars, satellites, and more...

They are invisible, so how do we study them?

Waves

Radio appears to behave like a wave.

Imagine throwing a rock into a lake...

Properties of Waves

Waves have two important properties.

  1. Length / Frequency

  2. Amplitude

Varying these creates endless possibilities!

Sound Analogy

How do we transmit information?

Ask if anyone plays a musical instrument.

We can think of radio waves like sound.

  1. Changing notes (frequencies)

  2. Changing volume (amplitude)

  3. Together, creates music

Antennas

How do we transmit information?

For sound, we use speakers and microphones.

For radio, we use antennas.

There are many kinds of antennas.

Key: antenna size is related to wave length / frequency.

Interference

Radio uses a shared medium.

What happens if everyone in the classroom is talking?

Solutions to prevent interference in wireless:

  1. Taking turns (raising your hand)

  2. Adjust power (whispering)

  3. Difference frequencies

Output Step
